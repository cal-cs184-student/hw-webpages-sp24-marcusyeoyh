Part II: Simulation via numerical integration

Describe the effects of changing the spring constant ks; how does the cloth behave from start to rest with a very low ks? A high ks?

A lower KS means a smaller spring constant, making the cloth seem less 'taut' and looser. On the other hand, a higher KS value means that the cloth looks more stiff.

This is observed where a cloth with a small KS value has many ripples visible as it falls, and the resting position at the end is lower than that of a cloth with a larger KS value, looking much looser. In comparison, the cloth with a high KS value has much more obvious creases in the cloth at its end position.
